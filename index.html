ABOUT ME

Welcome!

I am a medical microbiologist with knowledge in core microbiology, bioinformatics, sequencing, molecular biology, cancer research and clinical sciences. I am interested in the role of bacteria in the pathogenesis and progression of certain cancers. I also have a strong interest in applying machine learning and programming techniques to scientific research.

If you want to learn more about bacterial interactions, check out my research!

Maria Mempin, PhD


I received my Ph.D. from Macquarie University in 2019, investigating the interplay of host immune response, breast implant properties and microbial factors in a newly characterised neoplasm known as breast implant associated anaplastic large-cell lymphoma or BIA-ALCL.
Currently, I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow with the Surgical Infection Research Group (SIRG) at the Faculty of Medicine and Health Sciences, Macquarie University where I continue the work from my Ph.D. researching the role of bacterial pathogens and the microbiome in BIA-ALCL and in chronic implant infections.
